Quantum Optics¶

Quantum Optics Framework¶

From Professor Ivan Deutsch.

  1. Classical foundations

    1. Oscillators, interference, and coherence.
    2. Simple harmonic oscillators, quadratures, and Fourier analysis.
    3. Lorentz oscillator model.

  2. Quantum foundations

    1. Density matrix and coherence.
    2. Two level systems – Pauli algebra, Bloch-sphere, magnetic resonance.
    3. Quantum simple harmonic oscillator.

  3. Optical resonance for two level atoms

    1. Atom-photon interaction in electric dipole approximation.
    2. Pseudo-spin formulation, Rabi flopping.
    3. Density matrix formulation.
    4. Phenomenological damping – master equation and rate equations.

  4. The electromagnetic vacuum

    1. Quantization of the electromagnetic field.
    2. Spontaneous emission and Wigner-Weisskopf theory.
    3. Resonance fluorescence – Mollow triplet.
    4. Jaynes-Cummings model – Dressed states.

  5. Three level quantum coherence

    1. Raman resonance.
    2. Dark states and EIT.
    3. Slow light, fast light, and polaratons.

  6. Quantum-Optical Coherence

    1. Photon counting statistics – Mandel’s formula.
    2. Theory of partial coherence - Classical statistical optics
    3. Coherent states as quasi-classical states.
    4. Glauber’s correlation functions.
    5. Hanbury-Brown and Twiss interferometry.
    6. Bunching, antibunching ,and photon statistics.

  7. Nonclassical Light

    1. Phase space methods – Quasiprobability distributions, P-Glauber, Q-Husimi, W-Wigner functions.
    2. Nonlinear optics and nonclassical light.
    3. Squeezed states.
    4. Homodyne detection.
    5. Correlated twin photons.
    6. Photon interferometry.
